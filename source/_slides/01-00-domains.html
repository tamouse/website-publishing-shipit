Domains

What's a domain?

  • A domain is the name used to connect to your web server.
  • It is connected to the IP Address via the Domain Name Service.
  • Domains can be purchased from a registrar, such as Verisign, GoDaddy, Gandi.Net, or NameCheap.

There are 100s of places you can buy domain names from.

There used to only be a few top-level domains you could pick from. Now there are 100s, if not 1000s.

The most common are still .com, .org, and .net, and the two-letter country codes.

Popular new ones include .info, .name, .biz, and so on.

Concerns:

One highly popular TLD presently is .io, which is the country code assigned to the Indian Ocean Territories. This is under the directorate of the United Kingdom, and the occupants are primarily American U.S. military personnel.

The native people of the IO Territories were forceably removed from their islands by the British government in order to provide the space for the US military.

The native people are still petitioning to be allowed to return to their native land.

The native peoples receive absolutely NO funds from the licensing of the .io top-level domain. Think hard about whether it's worth it to you to support such an oppressive situation.

"Should I buy privacy services for my domain?"

Usually, the answer is "yes".

It provides real protection from people sniffing around, looking to hack your site, or steal your domian.

Privacy services cloak your personal information that is kept in WHOIS databases. This information is required by the ICANN in order to regular domains.

It is, however, publicly available, which means anyone can query the WHOIS database and obtain information about you as the domain owner. Such information include telephone numbers and mailing address.

The privacy service will cloak this information, giving you a chance to determine who can have access to it.

Caveats

Occasionally, having a privacy service can prevent you from moving a domain name from on registrar to another. This generally isn't a problem, though, and is something the service providers try to prevent.

Going with a privacy service from one of the big name registrars is usually a good thing.
